About Google
Delivering Google's products to our users requires us to develop technology across the entire
systems stack, from OS device drivers all the way up to multi-site software systems that run on
hundreds of thousands of computers. We design, build and operate warehouse-scale computer
systems that are deployed across the globe. We construct the networks that underpin and
connect them at a scale few others can match. We build storage systems that scale to
exabytes, approach the performance of RAM, and never lose a byte. We use large-scale
distributed systems to support code analysis and testing, making our developers some of the
most productive in the industry.
To do all this, we follow a hybrid research model (see Google's Hybrid Approach to Research,
Alfred Spector, Peter Norvig, and Slav Petrov. CACM July 2012) that deliberately blurs the line
between research and engineering. Instead of having a separate systems research team, our
teams build and operate large, production-quality systems. Our hybrid approach enables us to
conduct experiments at a nearly-unprecedented scale for research projects, and tackle
problems that few others can.
Google and Googlers are deeply involved in the networking and operating systems research
communities, in our work at Google, with our publications, and in our service to the research
community. We write and publish research papers to share what we have learned, and because
peer feedback and interaction helps us build better systems that benefit everybody. For
example:

Jobs at Google
Want to join us? Were actively looking for people to work on networking, distributed systems,
kernel and systems programming, and algorithms, amongst many other topics. At SoCC, stop by
our online whova booth, or drop us an email at socc-2020@google.com if you want to chat with
our researchers about the projects and opportunities at Google and how we solve interesting
problems for billions of people.
We often hire SoCC and OSDI attendees for internships and full-time jobs; please apply at
careers.google.com. For internships, apply now at careers.google.com/students dont wait
until 2021! (Undergrad and Masters students must apply by December 4, 2020; PhD students
should try to apply by that date, to improve their chances of finding a good project.)
You can find more information about networking-related jobs at g.co/research/networks; note
that our "networking" team hires a lot of distributed systems and computer architecture people.
